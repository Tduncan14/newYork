10 Facts About New York

1. New York City was originally called New Amsterdam when it was a Dutch colony.

2. The Statue of Liberty was a gift from France in 1886.

3. Ellis Island processed over 12 million immigrants between 1892 and 1954.

4. The Empire State Building was the tallest building in the world when it was completed in 1931.

5. Times Square is named after The New York Times, which moved there in 1904.

6. The NYC Subway is one of the oldest (1904) and largest in the world, with 472 stations.

7. New York City has five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island.

8. Over 800 languages are spoken in NYC, making it the most linguistically diverse city in the world.

9. Wall Street got its name from a wall built by Dutch settlers for protection.

10. Broadway features more than 40 professional theaters in the Theater District.
